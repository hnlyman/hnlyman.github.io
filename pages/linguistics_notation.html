Linguistics Notational Conventions

Univeral Conventions

  • italics indicate that the italicized text is being referred to, not used.
    • Wombat is a noun.
  • ‘single quotes’ enclose meanings of linguistic items.
    • German Zimmer ‘room’ is cognate with English timber.
  • /slashes/ enclose phonemes or phonological transcriptions written in IPA.
    • Wombat (/ˈwɑmˌbæt/) begins with the phoneme /w/.
  • [square brackets] enclose phones or phonetic transcriptions.
    • American with the cot-caught merger pronounce water as [ˈwɑɾɚ].
  • ⟨angle brackets⟩ enclose orthographic representations.
    • ⟨sh⟩ represents the voiceless postalveolar fricative /ʃ/ in standard English orthography.
  • *an asterisk represents an ungrammatical or impossible form, or a reconsructed form.
    • *She to the store went is ungrammatrical in standard English.
    • English sheep comes from Proto-Germanic *skēpą.

  • ǀ Dental
  • ǃ (Post)alveolar
  • ǂ Palatoalveolar
  • ǁ Alveolar lateral
Voiced Implosives
  • ɓ Bilabial
  • ɗ Denal/alveolar
  • ʄ Palatal
  • ɠ Velar
